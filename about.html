About Me

Personal Details

Hey there! My name is Frank Hui, and I'm a Canadian high school student who's passionate about computer science, graphics design, and photography.

My love of computer science originated in grade 7, when our teachers showed us Scratch, a beginner programming language aimed at young students. I fell in love with Scratch, and in grade 10, I moved on to Java with a course taught at our high school called AP Computer Science A and I scored a 5/5 on the AP exam. During that year, I also participated in Halite II a coding competition, and ended rank 33 out of 5,832 players worldwide. I also created a traffic simulator for a science fair project, earning me a silver medal, as well best Computer Science Award and best Mathematical Sciences Award at the Greater Vancouver Regional Science Fair. This year, I am focusing on creating a convolutional neural network that can detect brain cancer in patients early, for fast diagnosis and treatment. I've also attended 3 hackathons, MachoHacks 2018, hackseq 2018, and nwHacks 2019. At nwHacks, me, Andy Shen (inhostudios), and James Zhong (thebitspud) wrote a program that analysed walking patterns to identify the person walking, using the accelerometer in a phone. We ended up with the Wolfram Award, given to the Top 20 hackers at nwHacks. I am now investigating machine learning and web development. To check out some of my projects, visit the 'Coding' tab.

As I started Scratch, I noticed everyone had flat design logos, so naturally I wanted one for myself. A friend of mine introduced me to Inkscape, an open source vector design program. Ever since I've explored and found my way around it. I've now used Inkscape for over 4 years now, and I design logos for myself and others. I also make my own art, which you can see decorate this entire website. You can see some more in the 'Art' tab.

In addition to graphics design and programming, I also have a passion in photography. It all started when my grandfather passed down his DSLR to me, and my father taught me how to use it. It's been over eight years now, and ever since, every time my family has gone on a trip, I've taken pictures of the world around me. My dad upgraded to a Canon 6D, and then passed down his Canon 60D to me, which I now own and use. You can see some of my work in the 'Art' tab.

Even though I'm very busy during school, (I'm taking 5 APs! D:) I find make time to do what I love, and that includes writing fun code, making art and doing photography. To me, even though my academics may be very important, I'm pursuing high academic achievement to allow myself to have time for the things I love, so there will always be time for my family, arts, and photography.
